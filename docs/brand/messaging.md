# Messaging & Positioning

## One-liner

> **GigOn connects small businesses with trusted workers nearby, for the quick
> 1–3 hour jobs you need filled right now.**

## Positioning statement

For **small businesses and everyday workers in the Philippines** who need short,
local work filled or found _today_, **GigOn** is a **map-first gig marketplace**
that matches them with trusted people within walking distance — unlike scattered
**Facebook groups**, where there's no verification, no ratings, and no guarantee
anyone shows up or gets paid.

## The problem (the pain we're validating)

Daily, short-term work in the Philippines mostly happens in **Facebook groups
and group chats**. That means:

- **No-shows** — no accountability when someone doesn't turn up.
- **No verification** — you don't really know who you're hiring or working for.
- **No payment guarantee** — workers chase pay; businesses risk bad actors.
- **No proximity** — endless scrolling, then the person is across the city.

The landing page exists to test whether this pain is real and sharp enough that
people will **join a waitlist** to fix it.

## The solution — four pillars

| Pillar | Promise |
| --- | --- |
| **Proximity first** | Distance is shown up front. If it's not walkable, it doesn't waste your time. |
| **Two-way ratings** | Both sides rate every job, so reliable people rise and bad actors fade. |
| **Mutual check-out** | A job only closes when both sides confirm it's done and paid. |
| **Cash, direct** | Workers keep 100% of the agreed pay. GigOn never takes a cut from wages. |

## Audiences

**For business** — spas, restaurants, bars, and shops covering a rush or a
sudden no-show.
_Value:_ hands when you need them · only nearby workers · know before you book.

**For workers** — anyone with a spare hour who wants paid work a short walk away.
_Value:_ work near you · fill the gaps between commitments · paid on the spot.

The product is deliberately **two-sided**: the same map, built around speed and
trust, close to home.

## Message hierarchy (as built)

1. **Hero** — _Your gig is on._ + the one-liner + waitlist.
2. **Problem strip** — `1–3 hrs` and the Facebook-groups pain, reframed.
3. **Audience split** — business vs worker value, side by side.
4. **How it works** — Post → Match → Get it done → Both confirm.
5. **Why GigOn** — the four trust pillars.
6. **Final CTA** — _Be first in Cebu–Mactan._ + waitlist.

## Localisation notes

- **Market:** Philippines; **beta city:** Cebu–Mactan (`en_PH` locale).
- **Currency:** peso, `₱` glyph, no decimals on the landing (`₱300`).
- **Language:** clear English (the norm for PH urban business). Keep it simple
  so it reads naturally to Bisaya/Tagalog speakers; avoid heavy idiom.
- **Jobs framing:** short, walkable, 1–3 hours — cleaning, laundry, extra hands.

## Out of scope (for now)

Matching logic, identity verification, job completion/confirmation, and
payments/settlement are **deliberately not built yet** — they're referenced as
promises in the copy and will be designed after the waitlist validates demand.
The real app is planned **map-first** (Google Maps or Mapbox — see
[`apps/web/README.md`](../../apps/web/README.md)).
